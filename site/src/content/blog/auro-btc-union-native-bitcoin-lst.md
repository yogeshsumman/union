<p>Bitcoin LST/LRTs are rapidly emerging as one of the fastest-growing categories in DeFi, with combined TVL approaching $10+ billion as of date. By transforming idle Bitcoin into yield-generating assets, Bitcoin LSTs have unlocked new utility for the world’s most valuable digital asset, enabling it to participate in staking, lending, and liquidity provisioning across chains.</p>
<p>Existing Bitcoin LST products have unlocked some utility for idle BTC, but their isolated smart contract design limits native cross-chain scaling, secure custody coordination, and seamless DeFi composability. This means:</p>
<ul><li><p><strong>Custodial Risk: </strong>Many rely on centralized or semi-centralized custody setups, introducing single points of failure.</p></li><li><p><strong>Poor Crosschain Strategy: </strong>Many are locked to a single chain or use insecure/misaligned bridging solutions, hampering interoperability.</p></li><li><p><strong>Lack of Sustainable Yield: </strong>Most LSTs offer synthetic or rebased yield, not sourced from native Bitcoin staking or protocol revenue. Most rely on new chain tokens and airdrop farming.</p></li><li><p><strong>Low Adoption and TVL: </strong>Despite potential, user adoption remains limited due to low utility, unclear incentives, or integration barriers.</p></li><li><p><strong>Limited Utility in DeFi: </strong>Few LSTs have meaningful integrations in lending, yield, or collateral markets—restricting composability.</p></li></ul>
<p>In other words, Bitcoin LSTs depend on trustless interop protocols like Union to realize their full potential. So the question is: why shouldn’t crosschain L1s like Union be the distributor in the first place?</p>
<h2>Introducing: Auro BTC</h2>
<img src="/content/assets/4H21lQngrgH7h1dbKF3sho/LST-ANNOUCNEMENT-2.png" alt="Graphic showing Auro BTC in the middle, with chains around it—Ethereum, Babylon, Cosmos, Sei, and two question marks. In the top corner it says &quot;Bitcoin LST&quot;, and in the bottom corner it says &quot;Crosschain compatible, sustainable yield.&quot;">
<p>The Union Network and the Union Foundation are thrilled to announce the launch of <strong>Auro BTC</strong>, a Bitcoin LST that will leverage Union’s ZK-secured, trust-minimized interoperability layer to create a crosschain native asset that is composable, verifiable, and usable anywhere. Named Auro BTC after the Latin word for &quot;golden&quot; or &quot;gilded,&quot; it pays homage to Bitcoin’s status as digital gold.</p>
<p>Unlike existing LSTs that are siloed to specific chains or reliant on trusted bridges, Auro BTC will be instantly available across Ethereum, Cosmos, and other ecosystems with ZK-verified custody and mint/burn logic.</p>
<p>This means DeFi integrations, real-time yield distribution, and <a href="https://union.build/blog/crosschain-governance-by-union">crosschain governance</a> without relying on centralized relayers or oracles. Union’s architecture also allows for modular incentives, programmatic transparency, and a scalable custody model—all secured by Union's zero-knowledge message layer and growing network of chains.</p>
<h2>Bringing the Final Piece of the BTCfi Puzzle to Union</h2>
<p>BTCfi has been the centerpiece of Union’s <a href="https://union.build/blog/launch-interop-provider-babylon-genesis">alpha mainnet launch</a>, and introducing a Bitcoin LST product completes the missing piece of the <a href="https://x.com/union_build/status/1912867939251814833">Union BTCfi Flywheel</a>. Since Babylon Genesis’ launch, Union has transported over $40M worth of Bitcoin LSTs to the Babylon ecosystem, accounting for 99%+ of the total bridge volume.</p>
<p>The addition of Auro BTC strengthens the Union Network’s native asset strategy and unlocks a new revenue stream to fund the network's growth.</p>
