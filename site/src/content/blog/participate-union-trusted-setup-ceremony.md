<p>The next phase of Union’s <a href="https://x.com/union_build/status/1838984647772139678">Roadmap to Mainnet</a> has begun. The Trusted Setup Ceremony is an opportunity for everyone to contribute to Union, and ensure Union’s ZK-based interoperability is secure and decentralized.</p>

<p>Read on to learn about multi-party computation (MPC), Union’s Trusted Setup Ceremony, and how to contribute.</p>
<h2>What Is a Trusted Setup Ceremony?</h2>
<p>A Trusted Setup Ceremony, also known as multi-party computation (MPC), is a cryptographic process required for zero-knowledge proving systems like <a href="https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/on-chain-trusted-setup-ceremony/">Groth16</a> (used by Union) to function securely. It generates public parameters that are used to create and verify proofs without revealing sensitive data. This ceremony involves multiple participants to ensure no-one can compromise the system’s security. <strong>It only takes one honest contributor for the proving system to be secure.</strong></p>
<h2>Why Is This Important?</h2>
<p>The Trusted Setup Ceremony is crucial because it ensures the integrity and security of the zero-knowledge proofs used in the system. If the setup is compromised, malicious actors could potentially generate a fraudulent proof, undermining the protocol. By involving many unrelated and geographically distributed parties in the ceremony, it minimizes the risk of the entire ceremony consisting of bad actors.</p>
<h2>How Does the Ceremony Work?</h2>
<p>The ceremony involves multiple participants contributing randomness to generate the system’s public parameters. Each participant adds randomness, and after their contribution, their part of the randomness is securely discarded. As long as at least one participant remains honest and their contribution is kept secret, the parameters are considered secure for use in the proving system.</p>
<p>The contribution is done through <a href="https://ceremony.union.build">ceremony.union.build</a>, where contributors will be guided through running a dockerized application in their terminal that generates and submits the randomness. Both the ceremony website and docker app are <a href="https://docs.union.build/ceremony/">fully open source and reproducible</a>.</p>
<p>As part of the contribution, a GPG key is generated by the MPC client. The client uses this GPG key to sign the contribution before sending it to the MPC Coordinator. Contributors store their private GPG key, so that after the ceremony they can prove that they are the ones who generated their contribution. </p>
<p>After completing the ceremony, participants will have the option to publicly attest to their contribution. Please <strong>tweet your attestation</strong> to let the world know that Union’s ZK-enabled interoperability is decentralized, trustless, and secure.</p>
<h2>Two Phases: Private &amp; Public</h2>
<img src="/content/assets/2c9hviSQnlAO1iI0f2sDnO/PRIVATE-VS-PUBLIC-PHASE.png" alt="A graphical list of the differences between the public and private phase. Private phase requires a code, otherwise users can join the waitlist. Public phase is first come first serve, and people on the waitlist will receive an email ahead of time.">
<p>The Union Trusted Setup Ceremony will have two phases.</p>
<p>First will be the <strong>private phase</strong>. The purpose of the private phase is to allow protocols and contributors who are building with Union to participate. This aligns our builder community with Union by allowing them to contribute directly to the system’s security. It also allows the core team to thoroughly test the ceremony’s user flow ahead of the second phase.</p>
<p>If you’re not part of the private phase, you can join the waitlist at <a href="https://ceremony.union.build/">ceremony.union.build</a>. Anyone on the waitlist will receive an email 12-18 hours before the start of the second phase, also known as the <strong>public phase</strong>. This phase will be open to anyone, and will be first come, first serve. Those on the waitlist will have advanced notice of when phase two will start, but they won’t have priority. Make sure you’re online when the queue opens up. It will fill up quickly.</p>
<p>Only one person is able to perform the ceremony at a time, which means there are a finite number of contribution slots. Not everyone in the queue will get the opportunity to contribute.</p>
<h2>Explainer – How To Contribute to the Ceremony</h2>
<p>Full steps for contributing to the Union Trusted Setup Ceremony are below. If you have any issues, join the<a href="https://discord.union.build/"> Union Discord</a> and create a support ticket.</p>
<p><em>NOTE:</em> This guide is for users running <strong>macOS</strong> and <strong>Chrome</strong> or <strong>Firefox</strong>. Linux and Brave users can contribute as well, though different or additional steps may be required. Streamlined instructions for all supported operating systems and browsers are included in the ceremony—this is intended to be a more detailed guide. <strong>Windows, Windows Subsystem for Linux (WSL), and mobile devices are </strong><strong><em>not</em></strong><strong> supported.</strong></p>
<p>You will need to leave your computer online and <a href="https://support.apple.com/guide/mac-help/set-sleep-and-wake-settings-mchle41a6ccd/mac">awake</a>, with <a href="https://orbstack.dev/">OrbStack</a> and <a href="https://support.apple.com/guide/terminal/open-or-quit-terminal-apd5265185d-f365-44cb-8b09-71a064a42125/mac">Terminal</a> running, and the ceremony webpage open for the entire time you are in queue or actively contributing to the ceremony. Follow<a href="https://x.com/union_build/"> @union_build</a> for updates on the public phase.</p>
<p><em>Update:</em> A <a href="https://x.com/luknyb/status/1844096703223431686">video tutorial</a> is now available.</p>
